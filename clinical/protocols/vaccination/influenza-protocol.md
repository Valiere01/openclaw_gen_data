## 1. Purpose

This protocol establishes guidelines for influenza vaccination administration in accordance with CDC recommendations and Metropolitan Healthcare Network standards.

---

## 2. Eligibility Criteria

### 2.1 Universal Recommendation
- **All individuals 6 months of age and older** should receive annual influenza vaccination
- Vaccination should occur annually before onset of flu season (ideally by end of October)

### 2.2 Priority Populations
- Children 6 months through 4 years (59 months)
- Adults 50 years and older
- Pregnant women (any trimester)
- Residents of nursing homes and long-term care facilities
- Individuals with chronic medical conditions:
  - Pulmonary (including asthma)
  - Cardiovascular (except isolated hypertension)
  - Renal, hepatic, neurologic, hematologic, or metabolic disorders
  - Immunosuppression (including HIV)
- Healthcare personnel
- Household contacts of high-risk individuals

---

## 3. Contraindications

### 3.1 Absolute Contraindications
- Children younger than 6 months of age
- Severe, life-threatening allergy to any vaccine ingredient (other than egg proteins)
- History of severe allergic reaction to previous influenza vaccine dose

### 3.2 Precautions (Consult Provider)
- History of Guillain-Barré Syndrome within 6 weeks of previous flu vaccination
- Moderate or severe acute illness (with or without fever)
- Egg allergy (most can receive any flu vaccine; see Section 5)

---

## 4. Vaccine Types and Selection

| Vaccine Type | Brand Examples | Age Range | Administration |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|----------------|
| Inactivated (IIV4) | Fluzone, Fluarix | 6 months+ | Intramuscular |
| Recombinant (RIV4) | Flublok | 18 years+ | Intramuscular |
| Live Attenuated (LAIV4) | FluMist | 2-49 years | Intranasal |
| High-Dose (HD-IIV4) | Fluzone High-Dose | 65 years+ | Intramuscular |
| Adjuvanted (aIIV4) | Fluad | 65 years+ | Intramuscular |

### 4.1 Age-Based Recommendations

**6 months - 2 years:**
- Inactivated influenza vaccine (IIV4) only
- Volume: 0.25 mL (some formulations) or 0.5 mL depending on product

**3 years - 17 years:**
- IIV4 or LAIV4 (if no contraindications)
- Volume: 0.5 mL

**18 years - 64 years:**
- Any age-appropriate influenza vaccine
- No preference between vaccine types

**65 years and older:**
- **Preferentially recommended:** High-dose, adjuvanted, or recombinant vaccines
- If unavailable: any age-appropriate vaccine is acceptable

---

## 5. Special Populations

### 5.1 Egg Allergy
- Egg-allergic individuals may receive **any** influenza vaccine appropriate for age and health status
- No additional safety measures required beyond standard vaccination procedures
- Vaccination should be administered in setting where severe reactions can be recognized and treated

### 5.2 Pregnancy
- **Recommended:** Inactivated influenza vaccine (IIV4)
- **Contraindicated:** Live attenuated vaccine (LAIV4)
- Vaccination protects both mother and infant (passive immunity for first months of life)
- Safe in any trimester

### 5.3 Immunocompromised Individuals
- **Recommended:** Inactivated influenza vaccine (IIV4)
- **Contraindicated:** Live attenuated vaccine (LAIV4)
- May have reduced immune response; vaccination still recommended

### 5.4 Children 6 months - 8 years (First-Time Vaccination)
- **Two doses required** during first season of vaccination
- Doses separated by **minimum 4 weeks**
- If only one dose received in first season: two doses required in subsequent season

## 6. Administration Procedure

### 6.1 Pre-Vaccination Checklist
- [ ] Verify patient identity (two identifiers)
- [ ] Confirm age-appropriate vaccine selection
- [ ] Review contraindications and precautions
- [ ] Check vaccine expiration date
- [ ] Verify cold chain maintenance (2-8°C / 36-46°F)
- [ ] Obtain informed consent
- [ ] Document in immunization registry

### 6.2 Injection Technique (Intramuscular)

**Infants (6-12 months):**
- Site: Anterolateral thigh
- Needle: 1 inch, 22-25 gauge

**Children (1-2 years):**
- Site: Anterolateral thigh or deltoid
- Needle: 1-1.25 inch, 22-25 gauge

**Children (3+ years) and Adults:**
- Site: Deltoid muscle
- Needle: 1-1.5 inch (based on body mass), 22-25 gauge

### 6.3 Post-Vaccination
- Observe patient for 15 minutes (30 minutes for history of anaphylaxis)
- Provide Vaccine Information Statement (VIS)
- Document administration in medical record
- Report adverse events to VAERS if indicated

---

## 7. Documentation Requirements

### 7.1 Medical Record Documentation
- Vaccine type and manufacturer
- Lot number
- Date of administration
- Site and route of administration
- Name/title of administering provider
- VIS edition date and date provided to patient

### 7.2 Registry Reporting
- Report to state/local immunization registry within 14 days
- Include all elements per registry requirements

---

## 8. Storage and Handling

### 8.1 Temperature Requirements
- **Storage:** 2°C to 8°C (36°F to 46°F)
- **Do NOT freeze** (inactivated vaccines)
- **Protect from light**

### 8.2 Cold Chain Management
- Use dedicated vaccine refrigerator
- Continuous temperature monitoring with data logger
- Daily temperature review and documentation
- Emergency protocol for temperature excursions

---

## 9. Adverse Event Management

### 9.1 Common Reactions (Self-Limiting)
- Injection site: Pain, redness, swelling (10-40%)
- Systemic: Low-grade fever, myalgia, fatigue (1-10%)
- Management: Supportive care, antipyretics if needed

### 9.2 Serious Adverse Events (Report to VAERS)
- Anaphylaxis
- Guillain-Barré Syndrome
- Any unexpected or severe reaction
- Report within 48 hours of identification
