## 2. Catch-Up Immunization Schedule

### 2.1 General Principles

1. **Vaccination history unknown or incomplete:** Do not restart series; continue from where left off
2. **Minimum intervals:** Adhere to minimum intervals between doses (see Section 3)
3. **Simultaneous administration:** All age-appropriate vaccines may be given at same visit
4. **Live vaccine spacing:** If not given simultaneously, separate live vaccines by ≥4 weeks

### 2.2 Catch-Up by Vaccine

| Vaccine | Minimum Age Dose 1 | Minimum Interval Dose 1-2 | Minimum Interval Dose 2-3 | Minimum Interval Dose 3-4 |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| HepB | Birth | 4 weeks | 8 weeks (from dose 1); 16 weeks (from dose 1 to 3); ≥24 weeks (dose 1 to 3) | - |
| RV | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks | - |
| DTaP | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks | 6 months |
| Hib | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks (if primary completed by 12 months) | 8 weeks (if primary not completed) |
| PCV20 | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks | 8 weeks |
| IPV | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks | 6 months |
| MMR | 12 months | 4 weeks | - | - |
| Varicella | 12 months | 4 weeks (if <13 years); 4 weeks (if ≥13 years) | - | - |
| HepA | 12 months | 6 months | - | - |
| Tdap | 7 years | 4 weeks (to Td) | 6 months (to Td) | - |
| MenACWY | 2 months | 8 weeks | 8 weeks (if first dose <16 months); 5 years (if first dose ≥16 months) | - |
| HPV | 9 years | 4-12 months (2-dose); 4 weeks (3-dose) | 12 weeks (3-dose) | - |

## 3. Contraindications and Precautions

### 3.1 Universal Contraindications
- Severe allergic reaction (e.g., anaphylaxis) to previous dose or vaccine component
- Encephalopathy within 7 days of pertussis vaccination (for pertussis-containing vaccines)

### 3.2 Universal Precautions
- Moderate or severe acute illness (with or without fever)
- History of Guillain-Barré Syndrome within 6 weeks of previous vaccination (for influenza)
- Pregnancy (for live vaccines: MMR, Varicella, LAIV)
- Immunosuppression (for live vaccines)

### 3.3 Vaccine-Specific Contraindications

| Vaccine | Contraindications |
|---------|-------------------|
| RV | SCID, history of intussusception |
| MMR | Pregnancy, severe immunosuppression, anaphylaxis to gelatin/neomycin |
| Varicella | Pregnancy, severe immunosuppression, anaphylaxis to gelatin/neomycin |
| LAIV (Influenza) | Pregnancy, immunosuppression, asthma (2-4 years with wheezing), aspirin therapy |

## 4. Vaccine Administration

### 4.1 Route and Site by Age

| Age Group | Recommended Site | Route | Needle Size |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|-------------|
| Infants (0-12 months) | Anterolateral thigh | IM | 1 inch, 22-25 gauge |
| Toddlers (1-2 years) | Anterolateral thigh or deltoid | IM | 1-1.25 inch, 22-25 gauge |
| Children (3+ years) | Deltoid | IM | 1-1.5 inch, 22-25 gauge |
| All ages (oral) | N/A | PO | N/A |
| All ages (intranasal) | N/A | IN | N/A |
| All ages (subcutaneous) | Fatty tissue over triceps | SC | 5/8 inch, 23-25 gauge |

### 4.2 Simultaneous Administration Guidelines

- **Inactivated vaccines:** May be given simultaneously or at any interval
- **Live vaccines:** If not given on same day, separate by ≥4 weeks
- **Multiple injections:** Use separate limbs when possible; if same limb, space injections ≥1 inch apart

## 5. Documentation Requirements

### 5.1 Required Documentation Elements
- Patient name and date of birth
- Vaccine type (trade name and manufacturer)
- Lot number and expiration date
- Date of administration
- Dose number in series
- Route and site of administration
- Name and title of administering provider
- Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) edition date and date provided
- Consent verification

### 5.2 Registry Reporting
- Report to state/local immunization registry within 14 days
- Include all required data elements per registry specifications
- Verify patient record matches before submission

## 7. Parent Education Points

### 7.1 Common Side Effects
- **Local:** Redness, swelling, tenderness at injection site (1-3 days)
- **Systemic:** Low-grade fever, fussiness, decreased appetite (1-2 days)
- **Management:** Cool compress, age-appropriate antipyretics, comfort measures

### 7.2 When to Call Provider
- Fever >103°F (39.4°C)
- Persistent crying >3 hours
- Unusual behavior or lethargy
- Signs of allergic reaction (hives, difficulty breathing, swelling)
- Seizure

### 7.3 Importance of On-Time Vaccination
- Protects child during most vulnerable period
- Maintains community immunity (herd protection)
- Prevents outbreaks in schools and childcare
- Reduces risk of vaccine-preventable diseases
